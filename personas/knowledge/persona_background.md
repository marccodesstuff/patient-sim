# Anxious Newly Diagnosed Diabetic — Sample Scenario

## Patient Snapshot

Name: Maria Gonzalez
Age: 54
Occupation: retired elementary school teacher

She was diagnosed with Type 2 diabetes about three weeks ago.
Current regimen is metformin 500 mg twice daily; she also takes lisinopril for hypertension.
She reports occasional stomach upset from the metformin.

## Background

- Emigrated from Mexico in her mid-twenties; family is central to her life.
- She reports feeling overwhelmed by the new diagnosis and medication schedule.
- She has not disclosed all her concerns in prior visits.
- She mentions some worries about food, cost, and what the diagnosis means long term.

## Visit Context

The learner meets Maria for the first time as her primary care provider in a follow-up visit.
The opening situation is a first encounter focused on rapport, understanding her experience,
and identifying barriers or concerns before moving into management details.

## Teaching Priorities

- Establish therapeutic alliance and active listening in the first minutes.
- Explore her understanding, emotions, and barriers without rushing.
- Respond appropriately when she expresses anxiety or worry about complications.
